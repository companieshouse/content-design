abbreviations and acronyms

The first time you use an abbreviation or acronym, explain it in full on each page (unless it’s well known, like UK, HMRC and BEIS). Then refer to it by initials. For example, person with significant control (PSC). Do not use full stops in abbreviations: LLP not L.L.P.

Acronyms may be used in the first instance in certain circumstances, for example if it’s being used in a long heading.


accounts

Lower case.


agent assurance code

Lower case. Can be shortened to 'assurance code' after being used in full initially.


annual return

Lower case.


Anti-Money Laundering (AML) membership number

Title case for Anti-Money Laundering (AML), lower case for membership number. Explain the acronym at first use, then abbreviate to AML membership number.


Anti-Money Laundering (AML) registration

Title case for Anti-Money Laundering (AML), lower case for registration. Explain the acronym at first use, then abbreviate to AML registration.


appointed day

Do not use ‘appointed day’. Instead, either display the date (if you can), or explain that it is the company’s confirmation statement date.
